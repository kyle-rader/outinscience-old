What is The WWU Great Puzzle Hunt?
A puzzle hunt is like a scavenger hunt, with puzzles. Teams (4-6 is a nice size) travel on foot about campus (outdoors) solving a collection of puzzles (logic puzzles, word scrambles, trivia, etc.). Lots of prizes will be awarded. Whether your team places first or two hundred and fifty-first, competing in a puzzle hunt is a great way to stretch your mental muscles, bond with your teammates, and have a lot of fun!
When is it?
Saturday April 9th, 2016 @ 10:00 AM, Red Square
Who is invited?
All WWU students, regardless of major or puzzle-solving experience!  Sign up and create a team of up to 6 people, or register as an individual and browse other players and teams to bring a team together.
Do I have to be a math/science person to do well?
NO! A common misconception is that only mathematically inclined people are good at solving puzzles. In this puzzle hunt, it will be to your advantage to have people on your team with knowledge of music, art, humanities and social sciences, as well as science and mathematics.
How much does it cost?
This first year, it’s free! There will be a small fee for those who wish to purchase event Puzzle Master T-shirts. (More information to come.)
What if it rains?
You live in Washington... it rains here.
What should I bring to the puzzle hunt?
In case of rain bring a waterproof pack to hold:
  • Required: A smart phone. At least one person on each team should have a smart phone.
  • Notepad and/or clipboard and graph paper might come in handy.
  • Pencils, pens, highlighters
  • Scissors (enough for several people on the team to share). (Don't run with scissors!)
  • Scotch Tape. (Or duct tape, if you're a duct tape kind of person)
  • Umbrella... not for you, for the paper.
How long will the puzzle hunt last?
Registration and check-in will start at 10:00 a.m. in Red Square where your team will receive a rules packet and team T-shirts pre-ordered by March 18, 2016. The Puzzle Hunt will start at 11:00 a.m. when the first puzzle is released. Prizes will be awarded at approximately 3:00 p.m.
What kinds of puzzles will there be?
Example Puzzles
Each puzzle results in a code word or words that must be entered to stop the clock for that problem. Time is a factor, so make sure everyone on the team is assigned to a job. Some members can cut out objects if needed. If a problem has several parts, see if different members can tackle different parts independently.

Once the proper code word(s) has been entered, your team will be sent to the next destination. If your team is stuck, you may ask for hints. Each hint will add time to your clock. This information will be in the rules packet. Not all teams will travel the same paths. At each destination, you will need to scan a QR code to receive your next puzzle and re-start the clock.
